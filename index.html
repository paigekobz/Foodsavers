Who We Are

Every year approximately one third of all food produced for human consumption in the world (1.3 billion tonnes); gets etiher lost or wasted !

Annually, consumers in first world countries waste almost as much food as the whole net production of Sub-Sahara Africa (230 million tonnes)!

Even if you did know these facts prior to this website, you have to admit how much food we, as a society, are wasting. Think back to every time you or your family threw something away: how could you have prevented this? Does it ever occur to you how much of the food you buy is never finished? Well, that is what we are here for.

According to our extensive research, the main reason why people waste food occassionaly is because they buy more than they need. Overpurchasing has many causes such as: unorganized shopping behaviour, cheap prices, and the general packaging offered in many grocery stores. For example: many recipes may only need a portion of a vegetable, or just a single food; but most grocery stores don't sell individual foods, instead they sell wholesale packages. Another big reason is the amount of whole sale prices offered in stores: people are more likely to buy an excess of things if they believe it's on sale.

When grocery stores order food for consumers, they overstock a lot. This means that they put out way more food than they expect people to purchase, leading to the waste of tons of perfectly good food.
